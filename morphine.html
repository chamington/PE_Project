Morphine

What kind of drug is this?

Morphine is a depressant. The depression occurs straight to the nervous system to ease pain.

What are the street names for this drug?

Morphine isn't known to have many street names. This is because morphine is mostly associated with medicine, and not much as a drug. This is mainly due to morphine being used as a medicine instead of a drug. However, it is a drug, which is addictive, and it has some street names. These street names are:

  • Duramorph
  • M
  • Miss Emma
  • Monkey
  • Roxanol
  • White Stuff

What are the effects right after taking?

There are some immediate effects of morphine, which include

  • Drowsiness
  • Slowed breathing
  • Constipation
  • Unconsciousness
  • Nausea
  • Coma

What are the effects after taking morphine for a long time?

As there are short term effects of morphine, there are also long term effects. These effects are:

  • Physical dependence and addiction
  • Decreased sexual drive or interest
  • Increased risk of overdose and premature death

How can morphine effect a teenager socially?

This drug does do some damage to a teenage socially. Most of the time, the teenager would end up having friends who are also into either drugs, theft, or both.

How can morphine effect a teenager mentally?

Morphine effects the amount of certain pleasure inducing chemicals in the brain. By stimulating the structures that release these chemicals, morphine acts to increase the amount circulating in the brain. In addition, there are naturally occurring opioids produced in the brain, called endogenous opioids. Morphine binds to the receptor sites for endogenous opioids and reduces the excitability of neurons. It is believed that this contributes to the euphoria experienced when using morphine.

How can morphine effect a teenager physically?

An addiction to morphine can cause impaired physical performance, decreased hunger, interference with the menstrual cycle, tolerance, and withdrawal symptoms from no longer taking the substance
