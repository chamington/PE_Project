Ecstasy

What kind of drug is this?

3,4-methylenedioxy-methamphetamine (MDMA) is a synthetic drug that alters mood and perception (awareness of surrounding objects and conditions). It is chemically similar to both stimulants and hallucinogens, producing feelings of increased energy, pleasure, emotional warmth, and distorted sensory and time perception.

What are the street names for this drug?

This drug is wildly used in raves, so the street names are quite rave like. The names are such as:

  • Molly
  • MDMA
  • E

What are the effects right after taking?

Ecstasy has short term effects, such as:

  • Impaired judgment
  • False sense of affection
  • Confusion
  • Depression
  • Sleep problems
  • Severe anxiety
  • Paranoia
  • Drug cravings
  • Muscle tension
  • Faintness and chills or swelling
  • Involuntary teeth clenching
  • Blurred vision
  • Nausea

What are the effects after taking ecstasy for a long time?

Not many people know about the long term effects of ecstasy, such as:

  • Long-lasting brain damage affecting thought and memory
  • Damage to portions of the brain that regulate critical functions such as learning, sleep and emotion
  • It is as if the brain switchboard was torn apart, then rewired backwards
  • Degenerated nerve branches and nerve endings
  • Depression, anxiety, memory loss
  • Kidney failure
  • Hemorrhaging
  • Psychosis
  • Cardiovascular collapse
  • Convulsions
  • Death

What are some more interesting facts about ecstasy?

Like any other drug, ecstasy has interesting facts

  • The popular nickname Molly (slang for "molecular") often refers to the supposedly "pure" crystalline powder
  • It was also used for military purposes, to augment certain effects of amphetamines before it was illegalized in 1985.
  • Empathogens is an entirely new classification for drugs with effects like MDMA because of its unusual ill effects.

How can ecstasy effect a teenager socially?

Ecstasy damages social lives of people because of the effects on the brain. This is because lack of judgement can cause people to do stuff they wouldn't do normally. This includes doing stuff that can damage relationships with friends

How can ecstasy effect a teenager mentally?

Once the pill or capsule is swallowed, it takes about 15 years for MDMA to enter the bloodstream and reach the brain. MDMA produces its effects by increasing the activity of three neurotransmitters (the chemical messengers of brain cells): serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine.

How can ecstasy effect a teenager physically?

Ecstasy usually produces a euphoric rush, usually within 20 minutes of swallowing it, which can last up to eight hours. There have been reported cases of symptoms lasting for as long as 32 minutes after using ecstasy. Users may feel more alert, energetic and affectionate. Ecstasy also suppresses appetite.
